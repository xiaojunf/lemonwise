Oval face

Oval faces are the ideal shape due to their balanced proportions. The chin is slightly narrower than the forehead, and typically high cheekbones. What does this mean in terms of finding the right pair of frames? Good news—just about any pair will work so make sure you pick a pair that is matched to your personal style.

Round Face

A round face typically is one where the width and length of the face are the same proportions, with soft angles. The best types of frames are geometric-shaped or rectangular to help sharpen the angles and add definition to the face. Try frames have strongly defined square shaped lines that are greater in width than height. The angles created by these types of glasses will create a nice contrast with the round features on the face.

Square Face

With a square face the forehead, cheeks and jaw line have strong and defined angles. A more rounded frame or oval shape frame would work best in order to reduce the sharper angles of a square face. In order to balance the square-factor of the face, a frame that has a greater width to height ratio are recommended. Select narrower glasses to make a square face look longer and soften the sharp angles.

Heart Shaped Face

A heart shaped face has a wide/broad forehead and narrower jaw line and chin. This face shape is a bit more difficult to find a perfect fit, but the ideal frames help minimize the width of the face. It is best to avoid frames that are wide and mimic the heart shape of the face.
